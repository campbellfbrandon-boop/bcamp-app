# BCAMP PWA — GitHub Pages Deploy Guide
# 5 minutes. No coding. Free forever.

## WHAT YOU'RE DEPLOYING
A Progressive Web App that installs on your iPhone home screen like a native app.
Files in this zip:
  index.html     — the full BCAMP app
  manifest.json  — tells iOS how to install it
  sw.js          — service worker (offline support)
  icons/         — home screen icon + splash screen

---

## STEP 1 — Create a GitHub account (if you don't have one)
Go to https://github.com and sign up. Free.

---

## STEP 2 — Create a new repository
1. Click the "+" in the top right → "New repository"
2. Name it: bcamp-app  (or anything you want)
3. Set it to PUBLIC (required for free GitHub Pages)
4. Click "Create repository"

---

## STEP 3 — Upload the files
1. On your new repository page, click "uploading an existing file"
2. Drag ALL files from this folder into the upload area:
   - index.html
   - manifest.json
   - sw.js
   - icons/ folder (drag the whole folder)
3. Scroll down, click "Commit changes"

---

## STEP 4 — Enable GitHub Pages
1. Go to your repository → Settings tab
2. Scroll down to "Pages" in the left sidebar
3. Under "Source", select "Deploy from a branch"
4. Branch: main  |  Folder: / (root)
5. Click Save
6. Wait ~60 seconds, then your URL appears:
   https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/bcamp-app/

---

## STEP 5 — Install on iPhone
1. Open Safari on your iPhone (must be Safari, not Chrome)
2. Go to your GitHub Pages URL above
3. Tap the Share button (box with arrow pointing up)
4. Tap "Add to Home Screen"
5. Tap "Add"

BCAMP now appears on your iPhone home screen as a full-screen app with no browser chrome.

---

## STEP 6 — Connect Google Sheets (in the app)
1. Open BCAMP from your home screen
2. Tap ⚙ Sheets in the header
3. Follow the 2-step setup inside the app

---

## UPDATING THE APP IN FUTURE
When we update the app here in Claude:
1. Download the new index.html
2. Go to your GitHub repository
3. Click on index.html → Edit (pencil icon) → paste new content → Commit
   OR drag the new file into the repo to replace it
GitHub Pages redeploys automatically within ~60 seconds.

---

## DATA STORAGE
- localStorage is used for fast local storage on your device
- iOS Safari respects localStorage on installed PWAs (home screen apps)
- Google Sheets sync backs everything up to the cloud
- localStorage on iOS PWAs is NOT cleared when you clear Safari history
  (it's treated as app storage, not browser storage — this is what you want)
